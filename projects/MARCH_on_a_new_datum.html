Context

Climate change is affecting our life. The New York City is a place suffers from extreme climate vulnerability, such as Sea-Level Rise, Coastal Flooding, Heat Waves and Urban Heat Island Effect.

This site, which is a surface parking lot now and was a thermometer factory before and still have mercury under the property, is located at the edge of the South Street Seaport District at 250 Water Street, one of the most prosperous commercial districts in the New York City featured with high-rise buildings, and is in the hightest-risk flood zone in Manhattan, facing the risk of sea level rise, of 40 centimeters by 2050 and 2-5 meters by 2010. The aim of this project is to design and build a new headquarters for the Civilian Climate Corps (CCC) to help Manhattan transition into a decarbonized world.

CCC is a new public institution in US aims to engage young people and unemployed individuals in meaningful work related to climate resilience, renewable energy, conservation, and ecosystem restoration. This commercial zoning designation allows for a Floor Area Ratio of 6.5 for community facilities with a maximum height of 36.5m with applicable setbacks rules.
